Denali (Mt. McKinley) — 20,310 ft

Denali is the crown of North American alpinism — a genuine high-altitude expedition requiring weeks on the mountain, full crevasse rescue competency, and the ability to manage serious objective hazard in remote, self-sufficient conditions. The West Buttress route involves hauling heavy sleds through the Kahiltna Glacier approach, establishing camps through 14,200 ft and 17,200 ft, and timing a summit push through unpredictable subarctic weather.

Mt. Rainier — 14,410 ft

Rainier is one of the most glaciated peaks in the contiguous United States and serves as a premier proving ground for high-altitude techniques. The climb demands competent rope team management, crevasse navigation, fixed line ascending, and the sustained endurance of a long summit-day push departing well before midnight. A critical stepping stone in Alaskan expedition preparation.

Ice Climbing

Technical vertical terrain on Alaska's walls and Colorado's frozen drainages.

Moose's Tooth — 3,000 ft Vertical

A serious Alaskan objective with over 3,000 feet of vertical ice and mixed terrain. The Moose's Tooth sits deep in the Alaska Range and demands complete technical self-sufficiency — building anchors, managing ropes on sustained steep ice, and navigating complex rock-ice transitions in a committing alpine environment.

Colorado Ice Climbing

Seasonal ice climbing across Colorado's premier drainages — Ouray Ice Park, Vail area flows, and Front Range gullies. Colorado ice provides a reliable training ground for building tool placement technique, crampon footwork, and anchor construction across grades from WI2 to WI5+.

Colorado Mountaineering & Volunteering

Year-round alpine travel on Colorado's high peaks and volunteer work in the mountains.

High Peaks & Mountain Travel

Colorado's 14ers and winter alpine routes provide a year-round training environment for technical mountain travel. Winter ascents of high-altitude terrain develop the navigation, weather-reading, and team management skills that translate directly to larger expedition objectives. Colorado mountaineering has been a constant thread connecting technical ice climbing development, peak bagging, and volunteer search-and-rescue preparedness.
