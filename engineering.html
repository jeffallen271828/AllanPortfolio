Engineering Approach

How I think about and execute hardware design — from first principles through field-ready documentation.

Simulation First

Every design begins in LTspice or SIMetrix before touching hardware. Loop gain, transient response, and worst-case conditions all validated in simulation.

Measure, Don't Assume

Control-to-output stability verified on physical hardware with VNA measurements. Custom magnetics characterized by hand — gapping, winding, and testing each core.

Document Everything

Field-ready work instructions, structured BOMs, and single-line diagrams produced for every industrial project — usable documentation for technicians, not just engineers.

Electrical Engineering Intern — The Boring Company

May 2025 – July 2025  ·  High-Voltage Substations · NEC Code Compliance · Industrial EtherCAT

Conducted design, documentation, and commissioning operations for a 1.1 MVA electrical substation at a The Boring Company tunnel construction site. Handled everything from 13.2 kV utility step-downs to robust documentation for field technicians.

  • 13.2 kV to 480 V Distribution: Traced the primary utility step-down utilizing high-voltage TJB termination points, vacuum circuit breakers, and fault isolation mechanisms.
  • Split-Phase System: Drafted and documented the transformer wiring scheme to step the 480 V bus down to a 240 V/120 V split-phase distribution, bonding the neutral conductor in strict adherence to NEC regulations.
  • Telemetry & Sensor Integration: Deployed methane detection, ambient temperature sensors, and thermal imaging cameras over an EtherCAT industrial network for real-time tunnel environmental telemetry.
  • Electrical Panels: Designed and developed low-voltage 480 V distribution panels including High Resistance Grounding (HRG) systems, GFCI integrations, and Variable Frequency Drive (VFD) setups, supported by detailed hierarchical BOMs.
